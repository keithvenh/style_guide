Our Story

End Bible Poverty Now is a global Christian missions initiative to fulfill the Great Commission through Bible translation, distribution and engagement. We train, equip and lead others in translation, publication, duplication, distribution and engagement with the Bible. We believe the end of Bible poverty begins in the heart of each individual, and results in the spreading of the Word of God to every corner of the earth. We can END BIBLE POVERTY NOW ! See yourself in the story.

Our Mission

We believe a Bible can transform a life. We will do whatever it takes to get a Bible to every individual on earth because every Bible is a seed planted.

Our Vision

We are one of numerous initiatives dedicated to the global, Christian missionary effort to fulfill the Great Commission through Bible translation and distribution. We are led by Loren Cunningham, founder of international Christian missionary movement YWAM, whose organization endeavors to translate the Bible into every language and distribute bibles into every home by December 31, 2020.

Our Logo

Our logo is a simple horizontal wordmark. It communicates with clarity and urgency.

Our logo needs space to breathe. When using our logo, leave room on top, bottom, left and right to allow our logo to stand out. Our logo should never look crowded.

A good rule of thumb for our logo is that if you wonder if you left enough space around the logo, leave more. Space around our logo should be used liberally.

Our Typography

Industry

Industry

Industry

Industry

Industry is for Headings

This is Industry. We use this font for headings. You may recognize it. We use it for our logo. We can use Industry Book, Industry Book Italic and Industry Bold to make our headings stand out from the rest of our copy.

Industry Bold Italic is NOT for Headings

We love our heading font, and we love our logo, but we want them to be different from one another. Therefore, we don't use Industry Bold Italic for anything except our logo.

Open Sans

Open Sans

Open Sans

Open Sans

Open Sans is for Body Text

This is Open Sans. We use this font for everything else. It is simple, clean and easy-to-read. For most website content we use Open Sans Light, while in print we prefer Open Sans Regular. For an appropriate amount of contrast, we use Open Sans Semibold on the web, and Open Sans Bold in print.

Open Sans is from Google

Open Sans is free on Google Fonts, and therefore can be easily integrated into all of our G-Suite tools, including documents, spreadsheets and emails. This means we can use Open Sans in all of our communication.

Our Imagery

Images are powerful. A picture can capture the imagination and allow the viewer an experience beyond what words can describe. Carefully chosen images communicate a consistent and intentional message. When selecting an image, it should match at least one or more of these criteria.

Young People, Distribution and Joy

Our images should feature Young People, Bible Distribution and Joy. Here is one image that captures all three in a single image.

Distribution and Beautiful Settings

Our images should feature people going into hard-to-reach and beautiful places to distribute Bibles. Beautiful scenery catches the eye and draws people in, allowing them to see themselves in the story.

Bible Engagement and Literal Imagery

Our images should feature people engaging with the Bible, because the goal is not just distribution but engagement. Our images can be literal, featuring a person literally reading the Bible, or a person literally distributing a Bible.

Inclusive and Optimistic

Our images should be inclusive of all types of people, regardless of race, nationality and gender. Our images should feature people from a variety of backgrounds, and should communicate an optimistic outlook on life.

Informative

Our images can be informative when necessary, though our preference is to let images speak for themselves. Infographics should be used sparingly, but when used should carry a sense of shock or "wow" to grab the readers attention. Infographics can also be used to more clearly explain data, and as a helper in a longer body of text.
